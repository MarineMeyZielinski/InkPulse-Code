Healing of a tattoo

The act of tattooing: how does the skin react ?

Tattooing involves depositing ink into the skin using needles. The ink is ideally placed at the junction between the dermis and the epidermis—in other words, just beneath the outermost layer of skin. This outer layer is made up of dead cells and serves as a protective barrier against bacteria. During the tattooing process, the first layer of skin is pierced repeatedly to allow the ink pigments to pass through. This layer is very thin and contains no blood vessels. Afterward, the pigment molecules remain trapped between these layers of skin once healing is complete. After getting a tattoo, the skin is superficially wounded, making the protective barrier more vulnerable. It is important to avoid exposing the area to bacteria and to support the skin’s regeneration for approximately 3 to 6 weeks during the healing process.

Travis and your tattoo artist will guide you through each step and answer any questions you may have—don’t hesitate to reach out to them!
